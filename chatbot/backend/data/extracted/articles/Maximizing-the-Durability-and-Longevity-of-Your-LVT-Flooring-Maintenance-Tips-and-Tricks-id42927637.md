# Maximizing the Durability and Longevity of Your LVT Flooring: Maintenance Tips and Tricks

Clean regularly Regular cleaning is one of the most critical factors in maintaining the longevity and beauty of your LVT flooring. Dirt, dust, and debris can accumulate on the surface of the flooring, causing it to lose its shine and luster. Sweeping or vacuuming the floor daily is essential to remove these particles before they have a chance to scratch or damage the surface. Using a damp mop or cloth to clean the floor with a mild detergent solution or a pH-neutral cleaner can also help to remove dirt and grime. It's crucial to avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners as they can cause damage to the flooring. Steam cleaners should also be avoided as they can damage the adhesive holding the LVT flooring in place. By cleaning your LVT flooring regularly and using appropriate cleaning tools and solutions, you can keep it looking new and shiny for many years. It's a small investment of time that can pay off in the long run by helping to prevent damage and the need for costly repairs or replacements. Use protective pads Protective pads are an essential accessory when it comes to maintaining your LVT flooring's durability and longevity. Furniture legs, chair legs, and other heavy objects can scratch or damage the surface of the flooring, which can lead to costly repairs or replacements. By using protective pads or felt pads under the legs of your furniture, you can prevent scratches and damage to the flooring. These pads act as a barrier between the furniture and the flooring, reducing the amount of friction and pressure exerted on the surface. In addition to preventing scratches and damage, protective pads can also help reduce noise and prevent furniture from sliding on the floor. This can be especially beneficial in commercial spaces where there is a lot of foot traffic and furniture movement. Protective pads are inexpensive and easy to install, making them a smart investment in the long-term maintenance of your LVT flooring. By using them, you can help extend the life of your flooring, saving you time and money on repairs and replacements. Wipe spills immediately Wiping spills immediately is crucial when it comes to maintaining the durability and longevity of your LVT flooring. Spills can cause stains and damage to the flooring, particularly if they contain acidic substances or harsh chemicals. The longer a spill sits on the flooring, the more likely it is to penetrate the surface, causing permanent damage. To prevent this, it's essential to wipe up spills immediately using a clean, damp cloth or mop. Avoid using abrasive sponges or scouring pads, as they can scratch the surface of the flooring. It's also essential to use a pH-neutral cleaner or a mild detergent solution when cleaning up spills, as harsh chemicals can cause damage. By taking quick action when spills occur, you can prevent stains and damage to your LVT flooring, keeping it looking new and shiny for many years. It's a small investment of time that can pay off in the long run by helping to prevent costly repairs or replacements. Use rugs and mats Using rugs and mats is an effective way to protect your LVT flooring from wear and tear, especially in high-traffic areas and entryways. Rugs and mats act as a barrier between the flooring and foot traffic, preventing scratches and damage. When choosing rugs and mats, it's essential to opt for those with a non-slip backing to prevent slipping and tripping. This is especially important in commercial spaces, where safety is a top priority. It's also crucial to clean and vacuum rugs and mats regularly to prevent dirt and debris from building up underneath. In addition to protecting your LVT flooring, rugs and mats can also add an aesthetic element to your space. They come in a variety of colors and designs, allowing you to customize the look of your space to suit your tastes. By using rugs and mats, you can help maximize the durability and longevity of your LVT flooring, reducing the need for costly repairs or replacements in the future. Avoid direct sunlight Avoiding direct sunlight is essential to maintaining the color and appearance of your LVT flooring. Direct sunlight can cause fading and discoloration, which can be difficult to repair or fix. To prevent this, it's essential to use blinds or curtains to block out direct sunlight during the hottest parts of the day. This can be especially important in areas with large windows or glass doors that let in a lot of natural light. If you're looking for a more permanent solution, consider using UV-blocking window film to protect your flooring. This type of film can filter out harmful UV rays, reducing the risk of fading and discoloration. By taking steps to protect your LVT flooring from direct sunlight, you can help prolong its lifespan and keep it looking new and vibrant for many years. It's a simple yet effective way to maintain the durability and longevity of your flooring, saving you time and money on repairs or replacements in the future. Trim pet nails Trimming your pet's nails is an essential task to prevent damage to your LVT flooring. If left untrimmed, your pet's nails can scratch the surface of the flooring, leading to unsightly marks that can be difficult to remove. Regular nail trimming not only prevents damage but also helps keep your pet's nails healthy and comfortable. To begin, use nail clippers designed specifically for pets and make sure to cut the nails just before the quick, which is the part of the nail that contains blood vessels and nerves. Cutting too close to the quick can cause pain and bleeding. If you're unsure how to trim your pet's nails safely, consult a professional groomer or veterinarian for assistance. In addition to trimming, placing a mat or rug under your pet's food and water bowls can also help protect your LVT flooring from spills and scratches. This not only keeps your flooring in good condition but also helps make cleaning up after your pet easier. Overall, taking these steps to protect your LVT flooring will help ensure a happy and healthy home for both you and your furry friend. Maintain humidity levels Maintaining consistent humidity levels is essential for preserving the integrity of your LVT flooring. Changes in temperature and humidity can cause the flooring to expand or contract, leading to unsightly gaps or even buckling. To maintain a consistent level of humidity in your home or commercial space, it's important to use a humidifier in the winter and an air conditioner or dehumidifier in the summer. The ideal humidity level for LVT flooring is between 30% and 50%, so it's important to monitor humidity levels regularly using a hygrometer. If you live in an area with extreme temperature or humidity fluctuations, it may be helpful to install a HVAC system that can regulate temperature and humidity levels automatically. This can save you time and money in the long run by preventing damage to your flooring and extending its lifespan. By taking steps to maintain consistent humidity levels in your space, you can ensure that your LVT flooring remains beautiful and functional for years to come. Use the right cleaning tools Using the right cleaning tools is crucial for maintaining the beauty and durability of your LVT flooring. Using the wrong tools can cause scratches, damage, or even discoloration that can be difficult to repair. To avoid damaging your LVT flooring, avoid using steel wool, scouring pads, or abrasive brushes. Instead, opt for a soft-bristled broom, microfiber mop, or vacuum cleaner with a soft brush attachment to clean your flooring. These tools are gentle enough to clean without causing any damage to the surface. When cleaning your flooring, make sure to use a pH-neutral cleaner that's specifically formulated for LVT flooring. Avoid using harsh chemicals or solvents that can strip the protective layer of your flooring and cause it to lose its shine. By using the right cleaning tools and products, you can keep your LVT flooring looking beautiful for years to come. Regular cleaning and maintenance can help extend the lifespan of your flooring and protect your investment. Follow manufacturer's instructions It's important to follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and maintaining your LVT flooring. Every product is different, and the manufacturer's recommendations will ensure that you're using the right cleaning products and techniques for your specific flooring type. Before using any cleaning products or techniques, read the label carefully and follow the instructions. Using the wrong products or techniques can void the warranty and cause damage to the flooring. In addition to cleaning, it's important to follow the manufacturer's instructions for installation, temperature, and humidity levels. Failure to do so can also void the warranty and cause damage to the flooring. If you're unsure about the manufacturer's recommendations or have any questions about how to properly care for your LVT flooring, don't hesitate to contact the manufacturer or a professional flooring contractor for assistance. Schedule professional cleaning and maintenance Regular cleaning and maintenance can help keep your LVT flooring looking great, but sometimes it's necessary to schedule professional cleaning and maintenance services. Even with regular cleaning, dirt and grime can accumulate over time, and stubborn stains can be difficult to remove. Professional cleaners use specialized equipment and cleaning solutions to deep clean your flooring and remove tough stains and dirt. They also have the expertise to identify and repair any issues that may be affecting the performance or appearance of your flooring. It's recommended to schedule professional cleaning and maintenance services every six months to a year, depending on the level of traffic in your space. This can help extend the lifespan of your flooring and ensure that it looks and functions its best. In addition to professional cleaning, it's also important to schedule regular maintenance services, such as resealing or refinishing, to protect your flooring from wear and tear and extend its lifespan. By scheduling professional cleaning and maintenance services, you can keep your LVT flooring looking beautiful and functioning properly for years to come. In conclusion, maintaining the durability and longevity of your LVT flooring requires regular cleaning, using protective pads, wiping spills immediately, using rugs and mats, avoiding direct sunlight, trimming pet nails, maintaining humidity levels, using the right cleaning tools, following manufacturer's instructions, and scheduling professional cleaning as needed. By following these simple steps, you can extend the life of your LVT flooring and keep it looking as good as new for years to come. Investing in the proper maintenance of your LVT flooring not only saves you time and money in the long run but also ensures that your home or business always looks its best. Remember, taking care of your LVT flooring is a small price to pay for the benefits it provides, such as durability, comfort, and beauty. So, make it a habit to follow the maintenance tips mentioned above, and enjoy your beautiful LVT flooring for many years to come!
