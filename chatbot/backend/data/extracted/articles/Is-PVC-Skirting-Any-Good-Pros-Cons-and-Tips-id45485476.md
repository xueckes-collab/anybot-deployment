The Different Types of Skirting for Flooring When it comes to skirting for flooring, you have a few popular options: timber, PVC, and MDF. Each type comes with its unique benefits and considerations. Let's explore these in detail. Timber Skirting Timber skirting is a classic choice. It's known for its natural look and durability. Timber skirting can be made from various types of wood, including oak, pine, and mahogany. One huge benefit is its ability to be stained or painted to match the decor. Wood also offers a warm and traditional appearance that many homeowners love. However, be mindful that timber can be susceptible to moisture and may require more maintenance. Typical profiles include the elegant Ogee and the simple Bullnose designs. While it can be pricier than other materials, its charm often justifies the cost. PVC Skirting PVC skirting is a modern alternative to timber. It's made from a type of plastic called polyvinyl chloride. A significant advantage of PVC skirting is its water resistance, making it perfect for areas prone to dampness like bathrooms and kitchens. PVC skirting is low maintenance. It doesn't warp, rot, or require repainting. Plus, it’s affordable, making it a budget-friendly option. You can find PVC skirting in various colors and finishes, including wood grain effects, which is great for versatility. Due to its light weight, it’s also easy to install. So, if you're looking for something that's practical and fuss-free, PVC might be the way to go. MDF Skirting MDF (Medium Density Fiberboard) skirting is another versatile and budget-friendly option. Made from wood fibers and resin, MDF is a popular choice for modern home designs. One of its primary benefits is its smooth surface, which makes it easy to paint and customize. MDF is perfect if you want a uniform finish in your home. While it’s not as water-resistant as PVC, it isn’t prone to the same level of warping as timber. Just avoid using it in high-humidity areas. Profiles range from sleek Chamfer to decorative Ovolo, giving you plenty of choices. While MDF may not have the same prestige as solid wood, its flexibility and cost-effectiveness make it a strong contender. Frequently Asked Questions PVC skirting boards are a popular choice for many homeowners due to their durability, moisture resistance, and cost-effectiveness. Let’s address some common questions related to PVC skirting boards. What are the pros and cons of using PVC skirting boards? PVC skirting comes with several advantages. It is moisture-resistant, making it perfect for areas like bathrooms and kitchens. It's also easy to clean and maintain. On the downside, it may lack the natural warmth and aesthetic of timber. Can PVC skirting be a durable choice for homes? Absolutely. PVC skirting boards are known for their durability. They hold up well in high-traffic areas and don't scuff easily. Plus, any marks can be wiped away, making them ideal for families with children. How does MDF compare to PVC when choosing skirting materials? MDF skirting boards are less expensive and can be painted easily, but they are not as moisture-resistant as PVC. On the other hand, PVC is more durable and can withstand moisture and scratches, making it a better choice for long-term use in wet areas. Is it possible to paint PVC skirting boards for a custom color match? Yes, you can paint PVC skirting boards. Use a primer suitable for plastic before applying your chosen paint. This allows you to customize the color of your skirting to match your home’s decor. Where can I find the best variety of PVC skirting options? You can find a wide variety of PVC skirting options at home improvement stores, specialty building material shops, and online retailers. Brands like Orlandi and PBS offer a broad selection. In terms of strength and style, how does PVC skirting measure up against traditional materials?
