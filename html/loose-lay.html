Loose Lay LVT
Loose Lay Luxury Vinyl has been the most popular flooring option for commercial as well as residential interiors. This popularity stems from the Loose Lay’s coupling of luxury vinyl with phthalate-free plasticizers, thereby adding weight and durability to the LVT flooring.

Loose Lay is an easy to install LVT that requires adhesive for the perimeter tiles only. The LVT pieces within this glued perimeter are simply ‘laid’ down without any adhesive. The traction needed to hold the tiles at its place without any adhesive is provided by the added weight and the specialized backing. 

Availability of Loose Lay LVT in a variety of designs imitating natural patterns and vibrant colours can help you complete a splendid interior look in a cost effective way!

Is Loose Lay LVT best for acoustics?

Loose Lay LVT’s specialized backing is designed to absorb sound and provide additional comfort. It has been tested for Impact Insulation Class (IIC) and has been given a rating of 64, higher than the industry average. It is important to mention that IIC is a reliable measure for measurement of noise between the floor and the ceiling. Loose Lay provides more sound reduction compared to other flooring options. This makes it suitable for multi-family apartment complexes and commercial buildings having heavy footfall.

Do Loose Lay vinyl planks shrink?

Loose Lay does not shrink with moisture changes. The material used in this LVT is moisture expansion or contraction resistant. Any change in humidity levels in area with Loose Lay flooring will not affect the tiles. This makes it an ideal flooring solution for interiors having high probability of moisture variation and dampening like basements, kitchens & bathrooms.

How Loose Lay LVT holds onto subfloor without an adhesive?

Unlike other flooring options, Loose Lay neither requires any adhesive or staples to stick to the subfloor nor is it a click to lock flooring. Loose Lay is specially designed with such materials that help improve the friction which helps the tiles hold onto the subfloor. Only the Loose Lay tiles covering the perimeter of the installation are glued with adhesive while the area within the perimeter is simply covered with Loose Lay planks or tiles.

Is Loose Lay flooring waterproof?

Loose Lay flooring is perfectly waterproof provided that the flooring has been installed correctly. This makes it a perfect flooring choice for interiors having some kind of liquid spillage.

How long Loose Lay floor remains durable?

Loose Lay floor can remain like new for longer periods of time, provided that they are appropriately taken care of and maintained. These floors can hold up for years without wearing or buckling. 

How to install loose lay LVT?

Loose Lay LVT can be installed in following simple steps:

  Store the boxes containing Loose Lay LVT in a neatly stacked manner and avoid direct sunlight.
  Create a suitable environment for installation by acclimatizing the installation at 18° C to 29° C for 48 hours before and after the installation, and by ensuring that the subfloor is clean & dry.
  Sort visual variation of Loose Lay LVT on at least 25% of the floor and categorize visual variation piles. For LVT’s having arrows on the back, ensure that all arrows point in the same direction. This will help create a consistent blend of flooring and avoid image variations.
  Spread adhesive on the perimeter of the installation area. Take care that adhesive be applied only on the area that can be worked within the recommended working time recommended by the adhesive manufacturer.
  Install next rows according to the desired pattern that can be monolithic, quarter turn, half drop, parallel random, or Herringbone.
  Ensure that all tiles or planks are well set and flat with tight edges.
  Clean the residual adhesive from the perimeter planks or tiles with a clean, damp cloth.
  Thoroughly clean the LVT floor after 24 hours of installation with a cloth or mop damped with neutral pH cleaner.

Can Loose Lay LVT be uninstalled?

Unlike other flooring options, Loose Lay LVT can easily be uninstalled as it is not backed by adhesives (except for the perimeter). Not only can it be removed easily, but the removed tiles can be re-installed at other locations as well.

How to Repair Loose Lay LVT floor?

In case of stain, scratch, or buckling, Loose Lay LVT floor require repairing of the affected area only. In extreme cases of severe damage, entire section of flooring may require repairing. In any case, to repair the floor, consider the following:

  Gently remove the affected tiles.

 Use removed tiles as a guide to cut new pieces to be installed.

 Install the new pieces into place.

Always keep some extra boxes of Loose Lay LVT when installing the floor initially. This will help bring about repairs without having to procure new stacks of LVT tiles or planks.

How to increase life of Loose Lay LVT floor?

You can easily increase the life of Loose Lay LVT by taking following precautions:

  Avoid direct application soapy water, denatured alcohol or spirit to the floor.

 Avoid using vacuums with bristle beater bars for cleaning purposes.

 Use damp cloth to clean stains and dust. Always use a neutral pH solution to mop the floor.

 Do not rub aggressively or scourge to remove stains.

 Use felt pads beneath heavy furniture, appliances and equipment to prevent any scratch.

 Do not move heavy furniture, appliances and equipment directly on the floor. Prefer uplifting these or using appropriate furniture movers to do the same.
