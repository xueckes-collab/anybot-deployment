What is SPC Floor?

SPC rigid vinyl flooring (Rigid Core LVP) is the latest upgrade and improvement of
the Luxury Vinyl Tiles (LVT).It’s in fact considered to be the new trend in floor covering.The main content of spc are natural limestone powder, polyvinyl chloride and stabilizer which combined by a certain ratio to provide us a very stable composite material.

It is made up of 6 layers which includes the PVC Balance Sheet (virgin PVC), the
fiberglass layer, PVC middle layer with unilin click, decor printing film with excellent
design, wear layer with super strong vinyl, and of course the UV protective layer.
 
Other than the fact that it is more durable than a traditional vinyl flooring,SPC rigid vinyl flooring is also indentation resistant and 100% waterproof which is  very ideal for kitchens and bathrooms.Also, take note that SPC rigid vinyl flooring can be easily installed on different types of floor base.It’s formaldehyde free, completely safe flooring covering materials for both residential and public environment.

  • What is SPC floor?

    Stone Plastic Composite or popularly known as SPC, it is the newest innovation in the flooring market today.  It is made up of natural lime stone powder, poly vinyl chloride and stabilizers that make it incredibly rigid, scratch proof, waterproof, non flammable flooring which is a perfect choice for high traffic areas like bathroom, kitchen and basement. Studies show that SPC floorings do not produce formaldehyde and radioactive substance, making it eco-friendly and suitable for both commercial and home applications.

  • What are the benefits of SPC floor?

    • Design
    SPC uses advanced technology in design and printing which gives a crisp and realistic look of wood, stone or other concrete surfaces. It is widely available in numerous design, color and pattern that allow consumers to customize their home efficiently.

    • Waterproof
    Waterproof feature is one of the reasons making SPC the best choice in its class. This is because of its vinyl resin material that makes it resistant to water and molding. Unlike other floors, taking hardwood as an example, which is easily subjected to wear and tear. Consumers can install this to wet-prone areas like bathroom, laundry room and the kitchen. All in all, it is a perfect choice for environment that is subject to moisture, temperature and environment change.

    • Easy Installation
    In general, SPC are easy to install. Even without professional help, consumers can practically do it their own.
    They generally come in tile or plank form for easy set up, other types of SPC don’t even need messy glue in the installation process because they have built in adhesives for easy installation.

    • Affordability
    SPC are very cost effective. It is significantly less expensive than other luxury floorings. They are market favorite because of its versatility and easy DIY options, hence, helping consumers save more money from professional fees when installing.

  • What is the thickness of SPC floor?

    SPC’s flooring in general has a multi layer material; its thickness is ranging from 3.2mm to 9mm. Here are the layers of SPC flooring:
    • Wear layer: This transparent layer is responsible for its scratch resistant and easy clean feature.
    • Vinyl layer: This layer provides the decoration; this is where colors and patterns are printed.
    • SPC layer: This layer is the base of the SPC. It is the dense, waterproof and rigid.
    • Attached underlayment: This layer is generally made from IXPE or EVA foam, which provides sound insulation and cushioning.

  • How to inspect the quality for SPC floor?

    To determine if the SPC floor is of good quality or not, take into consideration the following criteria:

    · Color: The color of the high-quality pure material is usually beige. The bottom is generally the base material of the SPC, which is made up of polyvinyl chloride resin.

    · Look or appearance: Be keen on the surface of the SPC and check for its flatness and obvious stains and if the pattern is clear.

    · Feel: The SPC base material has a gentle touch and a smooth feel. Recycled materials and mixtures normally have dry feel on the hand because of lack of moisture.

    · Light transmittance: Raise floor in the air and use the flashlight of a mobile phone to illuminate from below the floor. If the top of the floor is bright, it entails high-quality SPC floor with good light diffusion.

    · Test the lock: Poor quality SPC floor are commonly brittle and easy to break. Whereas with high-quality SPC, the lock has reliable flexibility and does not break easily

    · Thickness: From the base material, color film layer, wear-resistant layer, and a UV layer, the actual measurement of a high quality SPC floor is 4.0mm.

    Strip resistance: Test the durability by trying to peel off the layers if they easily break when pulled apart from the base material.

  • How to maintain the service for SPC floor?

    · First of all, do not be in a rush to clean the SPC floor after the installation. It normally takes 48 hours for the planks to get firm and dry thoroughly.

    · Daily cleaning is a must for longer retention of the SPC floor, using a regular broom or a vacuum cleaner without beater bars to avoid damaging the flooring.

    · For wet cleaning, wash damp cloth or mop using mild detergents or floor cleaners with no harsh, abrasive components and strong chemical substance.

    · Avoid using steam cleaners since excessive heat and moisture could seep through the edges of the SPC flooring. This weakens adhesive bonds and layers of the flooring.

    · Reduce abrasion by using non-staining protector under your furniture and fixtures like chairs, sofa, cabinets and tables to further prevent dents and floor damage

    · Be sure to clean spills immediately.

    · Avoid floor exposure to direct sunlight to avoid fading or discoloring.

    · Apply SPC floor polish to keep its glossy look.

  • How to install the herringbone SPC floor?

    · Prepare the following tools for the installation: engineered hardwood flooring, border flooring strip, apron flooring strips, chalk line tool, circular saw, router, rubber mallet, wood glue, rafter squares, tape measure, spare piece of 3/4-inch plywood, screws, nailer, splines, flooring nails and painter’s tape

    · Before installing the herringbone pattern, check the subfloor and ensure it is leveled and completely even to refrain from starting from the top.

    • Find the center of the room using a tape measure and establish a border with a chalk line, creating a line that extends down the center of the room, from one wall to the other.
    •  Lay out the rectangular field that the herringbone pattern will occupy. If you’re going to include a border, establish it with a chalk line
    • Create a nailing blank to ensure that the planks line up properly. Draw a centerline down the middle of the nailing blank, and position the blank on the center chalk line, then screw it down.
    • Lay the first plank against one side of the nailing blank. Position the second plank against the other side of the blank to overlap the end of the first plank. Using a mallet and board, tap the planks firmly together. Continue placing two planks at a time to form the herringbone pattern across the room. Upon reaching the end of the first row, unscrew the nailing blank.
    • Continue moving the nailing blank and installing rows. Once done laying the entire floor, fill empty spaces where the nailing blank was, then trim each side of the floor with a circular saw to flush against the border line.
  • How many processes are there for SPC floor production?

    There are four (4) processes in the production of SPC floors.

     

    First is the mixture of raw materials. These are mixed altogether in the high-speed mixer with thermal mixing temperature of 125 ℃. After which, the mixture is cooled down   to prevent caking and discoloration with a cold mix temperature of 55 ℃.

     

    Second process is the extrusion, where the mixture undergoes heated extrusion into the sheet forming until the base material stick to the color film and wear-resisting layer. After which, cooling comes next.

     

    Third process is UV tempering with hot water temperature of 80-120 ℃. Then last steps to be done include cutting, notching, trimming, quality inspection and packaging.

  • What is difference between vinyl floor and SPC floor?

    Vinyl flooring is made up of simple synthetic PVC material, making it flexible yet durable. It has two common types: vinyl sheet flooring and vinyl tiles. These are commonly used in bathrooms and kitchens and are generally easy to install. However, vinyl flooring is prone to damage and quick wear and tear due to sharp objects and heavy foot traffic and load. This flooring cannot be recommended for outdoors, as well, due to extreme temperatures, along with too much exposure to sunlight which cause color fading and wearing out of the vinyl layer over the course of time.

     

    SPC (stone plastic composite or stone polymer composite) flooring is a form of vinyl but has rigid composite engineering known for providing 100% waterproof feature and unmatched durability, while making floors look luxurious with its natural wood and stone appearance. This flooring type is one of the latest in flooring technology as it boasts both functional and aesthetic attributes, along with practicality and esteemed strength. The rigid core of SPC floors is composed of limestone powder, polyvinyl chloride and stabilizers. This type of floor is denser and can withstand heavy impacts, perfect for high foot traffic environment.

  • Why is SPC floor so popular in the world?

    SPC flooring soared high in popularity due to its outstanding features such as its multi-layer coating, which makes it able to endure heavy loads and high-traffic areas in residential and commercial units. Its rigid core composite makes it exceptional among any other flooring, as it provides instant elegance that exudes through the real-looking wood and stone mimic giving your home timeless ambience.

     

    SPC floor structure includes the following layers:

    · UV Coating: The top/surface layer of an SPC floor is designed to prevent discoloration and micro scratches.

    · Wear Layer: This layer protects the vinyl from wear and tear, scratches, stains and frequent foot traffic.  It is the transparent top coating applied to the vinyl plank.

    · Vinyl Top Coat Layer: This layer defines the physical appearance of the floor, providing wide variety of colors, patterns, design and styles, as it copies the exquisite look of natural hardwood. This also serves as the primary aesthetic layer, projecting the pattern, texture and total appearance of the flooring

    · SPC Core Base Layer: This layer is the rigid core that is dense and waterproof, ensuring that the shape of vinyl floor planks is well maintained. Its core is engineered with the combination of limestone and stabilizers, creating a reinforced tough core

    · Underlayment/Base Layer: The base layer has attached underlayment, making installation easier and has both vapor and sound barrier capabilities.

     

    SPC flooring boasts wide range of design and style and can even last for two decades if it is well-maintained. So in terms of durability, longevity, and DIY-friendly installation, SPC flooring makes a great choice.

  • What is micro bevel?

    Micro bevel is a type of edge style which is commonly used in the industry because of its versatility in enhancing home designs and adding floor depth and dimension with the shadows they create. It is a subtle, 45-degree angles found along the edges of plank flooring. The bevels form a v-shape where planks connect to define each plank. These have become the standard in pre-finished wood flooring.

  • What is difference between micro bevel and painted bevel？

    Micro bevels are tiny, light bevels that provide a subtle hint of plank definition. A bevel on a floorboard is a small angled cut which goes along its edges. Micro bevel has very small bevel, making it less noticeable and is often used to hide unequal heights of pre-finished floorboards.  Micro bevel options include eased, kissed, micro-v, and painted micro-bevel.

    Painted bevels are emphasized with color-matched paint to enhance plank definition to make the floor look very realistic. Using a distinct machine, the edges are painted to complement floor color and add dimension that highlights each board’s look and expression.

Direct contact with strong chemicals may also cause yellowing and discoloration on the SPC floor surface, depending on the quality of the installed SPC.

  • How to repair SPC floor?

    Due to improper maintenance and external factors, planks can be damaged, may get scratched due to daily wear and tear and will even call for replacement for worse situations.

     

    It is very important to ensure that the floor is clean, free from loose surface dirt prior to starting repairs. Use soft bristle broom and damp mop or rug cloth washed with mild detergent and floor cleaners, and then let the floor dry thoroughly.

     

    For scratches on the plank, a quality car wax can be used to restore the original shine of the vinyl surface. Fill the entire scratch with the wax, carefully wiping away any excess wax from the area. After waxing, wipe with a clean cloth and gently rub it over the area for a smooth and clean finish.

     

    If the plank is damaged and needs replacing, elevate the molding of the wall, disconnect the tongue of one plank from the groove of the connecting one, replace the damaged plank and re-lock the planks that you have just removed. Afterwards, re-install the molding of the wall to its original place.

  • Is SPC floor waterproof?

    Yes, it is waterproof. The main material of the SPC floor is vinyl resin and its core is highly water-resistant due to the durable wear film on its surface that serves as a protective layer.

  • What is the difference between WPC and SPC vinyl flooring?

    WPC (wood-plastic composite) vinyl flooring is best known for being 100% waterproof due to its waterproof core. This flooring boasts traditional hardwood with low maintenance requirement and has become one of the most popular flooring options for commercial spaces and businesses. It has added foaming agent to increase flexibility and comfort underfoot. However, WPC flooring has lesser durability compared with SPC flooring due to its softer core, entailing dents and damage if something heavy drops on it. In terms of affordability, WPC is more expensive than SPC flooring.

     

    SPC flooring or the rigid core luxury vinyl flooring is composed of natural lime stone powder, poly vinyl chloride and stabilizers. This rigid composition provides SPC flooring with ultra-tough core, making it more durable, even on uneven subfloors.

  • What is the difference between laminate floor and SPC flooring?

    Fiberboard or compressed wood fiber is the core material used for laminate flooring, which can be classified to medium density fiberboard (MDF) or high density fiberboard (HDF) depending on the quality. This composition makes laminate flooring susceptible to water or moisture. Due to lack of the waterproof feature, laminate flooring is only used in living rooms and bedrooms.

     

    SPC flooring has rigid composite materials that make it more durable than laminate flooring. Its high density feature makes it strong enough to sustain heavy foot traffic and resist water or any liquid spillage and is therefore suitable in any part of the house, most especially in wet-prone areas such as the bathroom and kitchen.

  • How much does SPC flooring cost?

    Pricing depends on the type of SPC flooring. Traditional ones cost as low as $6.6 per sqm, while medium ranged SPC flooring is around $8.00 per sqm. High end SPC flooring like luxury SPC plank are pricey due to its defined high quality and, thus, range from $9 to $10 per sqm.
